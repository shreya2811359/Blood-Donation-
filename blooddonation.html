Introduction

Blood donation is one of the most valuable acts of kindness that a person can perform. Every day thousands of patients require blood due to accidents, surgeries, childbirth complications, and serious diseases such as cancer and anemia.

Blood cannot be manufactured artificially, therefore hospitals depend completely on voluntary blood donors to maintain a safe blood supply. By donating blood, a person can help save lives and support the healthcare system.

Why Blood Donation is Important

Save Lives

A single blood donation can save up to three lives because the blood is separated into different components such as red cells, plasma and platelets.

Emergency Support

Blood is essential during accidents, surgeries and natural disasters. Hospitals must always have a safe supply of blood available.

Community Service

Donating blood is a simple act that shows care and responsibility towards society and helps people in need.

Blood Donation Process

  1. Registration of the donor
  2. Medical checkup and health screening
  3. Collection of blood using sterile equipment
  4. Rest and refreshments after donation

After donation, the collected blood is tested and stored in blood banks. It is later supplied to hospitals where it is used to treat patients.
